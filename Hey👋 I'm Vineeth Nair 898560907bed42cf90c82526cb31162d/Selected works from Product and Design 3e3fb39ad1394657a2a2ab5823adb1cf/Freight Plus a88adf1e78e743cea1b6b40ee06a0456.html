Problem Statement

Indian Logistics Supply market (trucking) is a very fragmented market and there is no generalization or standards in terms of processes, pricing or service level.

Shippers:

1. Normal practice: Shippers would use freight brokers as a middle man to match them with available truckers, taking a commission in the process.

2. Not Having Access to the Right Shipping Options and not getting the best price

3. Having Reliable and Accessible Shipment Tracking

Truck Companies:

1. Driver shortage due to lower pay

2. For trucking companies, the costs of running a business add up fast. From fuel, which can amount to 34% of trucking costs, to maintenance and tires, which together eat another 10%, these significant costs are an endless challenge. While some larger companies can offset these expenses by negotiating bulk discounts, most have to pay full price to keep their trucks on the road.

Market Research

🚚The trucking industry drew in revenues of $796 billion in 2018, with trucks moving 71% of the nation’s freight.

💹Reports predict the global freight trucking market to grow with a CAGR of 6.0% over the forecast period of 2018-2024.

Solution

Shippers:

1. Real-time tracking of goods transport and Geofence.

2. No need to wait for Freight for a long time.

3. Removes the need for a middle man.

Truck Drivers/Companies

1. Drivers also get information on what they are hauling as well as how much they’ll be paid.

2. Can find their next job right from the app

3. Easy billing and payment
